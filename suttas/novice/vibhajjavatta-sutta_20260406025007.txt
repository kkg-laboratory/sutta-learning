Vibhajjavatta Sutta

Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Blessed One was dwelling at Savatthi, in the Jets' Grove, Anathapindika's Park. Then a certain bhikkhu approached the Blessed One, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. This bhikkhu said to the Blessed One: "Venerable sir, when assigning and handling funds for the Sangha, how should we proceed so as to be in accordance with the Dhamma?"

The Blessed One said: "Bhikkhu, when handling funds for the Sangha, one should be mindful and reflect: 'These funds are not mine, but are given by the faithful for the welfare and happiness of the Sangha.' One should consider: 'Will the use of these funds bring benefit to the Sangha and conduce to the harmlessness of all beings?' One should be free from attachment, aversion, and delusion, and assign the funds with a mind that is generous, compassionate, and wise.

"Just as a wise Householder, when giving, gives with a mind that is not attached to the gift, nor to the recipient, nor to any result, so too, the bhikkhu, when handling funds for the Sangha, should give with a mind that is not attached to the funds, nor to the use of the funds, nor to any result. In this way, the bhikkhu is freed from the taint of worldly things and dwells in the freedom of the Dhamma.

"Moreover, bhikkhu, when handling funds for the Sangha, one should be transparent and open, like a pool of clear water. One should inform the Sangha of the income and expenditure of funds, and assign the funds with the consent of the Sangha. In this way, trust and confidence are established, and the Sangha dwells in harmony.

"Thus, bhikkhu, the wise and mindful handling of funds for the Sangha is a manifestation of the divine virtues, and is conducive to the well-being and happiness of the Sangha. It is a meritorious deed that brings joy and benefit to all beings, and is a cause for the increase of the Dhamma."
