The Timely Mind Sutta

Thus have I heard. On one occasion the Buddha was dwelling at the Jetavana Grove, near the city of Shravasti. The Venerable Ananda approached the Buddha, paid homage to him, and sat down to one side. The Venerable Ananda said: "Venerable sir, the monks wish to know how to cultivate mindfulness of time, so that they may make the most of the time they have, and not be deluded by the passing of time."

The Buddha said: "Ananda, the monk who wishes to cultivate mindfulness of time should reflect on the impermanence of all things. He should consider that time is constantly passing, and that every moment is an opportunity to practice the Dhamma. Just as the river's current flows constantly, never staying the same, so too does time flow, never staying the same.

"The monk should divide the day into four periods: the time before dawn, the time after dawn, the time after lunch, and the time before bedtime. During each period, he should reflect on his actions, and ask himself: 'Have I used this time wisely? Have I practiced mindfulness, and cultivated wholesome qualities?' If he has not, he should make a determination to do better in the next period.

"The monk should also reflect on the five aspects of time: the time of birth, the time of growth, the time of decay, the time of death, and the time of rebirth. He should consider how these aspects of time apply to all phenomena, and how they remind him of the impermanence of all things.

"By cultivating mindfulness of time in this way, the monk will become aware of the preciousness of each moment, and will make the most of the time he has. He will not be deluded by the passing of time, but will instead use it as an opportunity to practice the Dhamma, and to attain liberation from suffering."
